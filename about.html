About Bono Region Youth Parliament

Empowering the next generation of leaders

History of Bono Region

The Bono Region was carved out of the then Brong-Ahafo Region in 2019 as part of Ghana's decentralization efforts. It is rich in culture, agriculture, and history, with Sunyani as its capital.

The region is known for its vibrant youth population, agricultural productivity (cocoa, cashew, maize), and growing industrial sector. The youth constitute over 60% of the population, making youth development crucial for regional progress.

About Bono Region Youth Parliament

Established in 2020, the Bono Region Youth Parliament is a democratic platform that gives young people a voice in regional development. It operates as a shadow parliament, with elected youth representatives from all districts.

The parliament provides leadership training, advocates for youth-friendly policies, and implements community development projects. It works closely with the Regional Coordinating Council, National Youth Authority, and Parliament of Ghana.

Our Objectives

  • Youth Unemployment: Create job opportunities through skills training, entrepreneurship support, and partnerships with industries.
  • Education: Advocate for quality education, provide scholarships, and organize mentorship programs in schools.
  • Entrepreneurship: Support young entrepreneurs with training, funding access, and business development services.
  • Leadership Development: Train youth in leadership, governance, and civic participation.
  • Community Building: Implement community development projects and promote social cohesion.
  • Policy Advocacy: Influence youth-friendly policies at district and regional levels.
  • Health & Wellbeing: Promote sexual reproductive health, mental health awareness, and sports.
  • Environment: Engage youth in climate action and environmental conservation.
